Receipt Analysis Methodology
Reference guide for monthly receipt processing and Excel file preparation.
1. Data Collection
Collect all receipt images for the month from every store. Receipts may come as:
• Physical paper receipt photos
• Digital screenshots from store apps
• Online order confirmations
Target stores include Walmart, Publix, Target, Costco (in-warehouse and online), Austell Farmers Market, and Instacart (covering Sprouts, Ingles, Costco delivery). Upload all receipt images at once for processing.

New stores can be added at any time. If you shop at a new store in a future month, simply include those receipts — the dashboard will automatically pick up any new store names from the data. No configuration needed.
2. Data Extraction
Every line item on every receipt is extracted. For each item, capture the following data points into the base table:

3. Product Name Decoding
Receipts use heavily abbreviated product names. Each abbreviation must be decoded to its full product name. When a receipt abbreviation is ambiguous, use the UPC barcode number (printed on many receipts) to look up the exact product. For weight-based items (e.g., produce sold per pound), record the total line price as the Line Total.

4. Category Assignment
Every item is assigned exactly one category. Use these consistently across months for accurate comparisons. New categories can be added at any time — if a product doesn't fit an existing category, create a new one in the spreadsheet and the dashboard will automatically include it. You can also rename or recategorize items after uploading by clicking any item in the All Items tab.

5. Dashboard Classification Logic
The dashboard auto-classifies each item into one of three types:

Toiletry — Any item whose Category is "Health & Beauty", "Household & Cleaning", or "Paper Products" (regardless of the Non-Grocery flag).

Grocery — Any item where Non-Grocery = FALSE and it's not a toiletry category.

Other — Any item where Non-Grocery = TRUE and it's not a toiletry category (toys, electronics, clothing, etc.).
6. Spreadsheet Structure (6 Sheets)
Only Sheet 1 (All Transactions) is required for the dashboard upload. Sheets 2–6 are optional reference sheets for your own analysis.
Sheet 1: All Transactions (REQUIRED)
• Every line item from every receipt, one row per item
• Columns A through I as defined above
• Sorted chronologically by date
• Non-grocery rows highlighted in yellow (fill color FFF2CC)
• Final row contains SUM formula for column H (grand total)
Sheet 2: By Store (optional)
Pivot on Store. For each store: Total Items (SUM of Qty), Number of Trips (COUNT of unique dates), Total Spent (SUM of Line Total), % of Total Spend. Sorted descending by Total Spent.
Sheet 3: By Category (optional)
Pivot on Category. For each category: Total Items, Total Spent, % of Total Spend, Grocery/Non-Grocery label. Non-grocery categories highlighted in yellow. Sorted descending by Total Spent.
Sheet 4: Top Spent Items (optional)
Group by Full Product Name. For each unique product: Total Qty Bought, Total Spent. Ranked by Total Spent descending (rank 1 = most expensive cumulative item).
Sheet 5: Frequently Purchased (optional)
Same as Sheet 4, but filtered to items where total quantity is 2 or more. Additional column: Avg Cost Per Unit (Total Spent ÷ Total Qty Bought). Sorted descending by Total Spent.
Sheet 6: Summary Dashboard (optional)
Key metrics calculated from all data:
MetricFormula / Logic
Total Spend (All Items)SUM of all Line Total values
Total Grocery SpendSUM of Line Total WHERE Non-Grocery = FALSE
Total Non-Grocery SpendSUM of Line Total WHERE Non-Grocery = TRUE
Total Line ItemsCOUNT of all data rows (one per receipt line)
Total Items PurchasedSUM of all Qty values (accounts for qty > 1)
Number of StoresCOUNT of unique values in Store column
Number of TripsCOUNT of unique (Store + Date) combinations
Average Spend Per TripTotal Spend ÷ Number of Trips
Also includes: Top 10 most expensive items (by cumulative spend) and Top 10 most frequently purchased items (by total quantity).
7. Formatting Rules
• All currency values formatted as $#,##0.00
• Percentage values formatted as 0.0%
• Header rows: white text on dark blue fill (2F5496), bold Arial 11pt
• Data rows: Arial 10pt, light gray borders (D9D9D9)
• Non-grocery rows: yellow highlight fill (FFF2CC)
• Grand total rows: bold font
• Dates with asterisk (e.g., 01/25*) indicate estimated dates from damaged/unreadable receipts
