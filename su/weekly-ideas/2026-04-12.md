## 2. 📚 EDUCATIONAL: Scripting & Echolalia Explained
**Post Type:** Reel (45-60 sec)  
**Best Day:** Thursday, April 16  
**Best Time:** 9-10 AM ⭐ PEAK

**Hook/Headline:**  
"My kid only talks in movie quotes. Is that communication? YES. Here's why."

**Key Points:**
1. What is echolalia? (repeating words/phrases heard elsewhere)
2. What is scripting? (using memorized scripts from shows, books, conversations)
3. It IS communication - not "meaningless repetition"
4. Functional echolalia: using scripts to express real feelings/needs
5. Example: quoting "I'm scared" from a movie = actually being scared
6. Don't discourage it - decode it
7. How to respond: acknowledge the emotion behind the script
8. Over time, they may build original language ON TOP of scripts

**Script Outline (Reel):**
"When your kid responds to 'How are you?' with the entire intro to Bluey..."

(text on screen: "That's echolalia. And it's NOT nonsense.")

"Scripting and echolalia are ways autistic brains process and use language. They're borrowing words that work."

"Your job? Decode the script. 'I'm Bingo and I'm sad' might mean... they're actually sad."

"Don't shut it down. Listen deeper."

**Caption:**
"Stop quoting movies and use your own words!"

I used to say this. I was wrong.

Echolalia and scripting aren't random noise. They're your child using language the way THEIR brain processes it.

That movie quote? It might be the only way they can express something real.

Your job: decode, don't dismiss.

What scripts does your kid use? I bet they mean something. 💙
