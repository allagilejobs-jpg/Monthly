## 1. Educational: The After-School Crash Plan

**Hook/headline:** “If your child melts down after school, it may not be defiance.”

**Key points:**
1. Slide 1: Hook, “The after-school crash is real.”

## 2. Educational: Interoception, The Hidden Sense Parents Miss

**Post type:** Reel  
**Hook/headline:** “Your child may not be ignoring hunger, pain, or bathroom cues. They may not feel them clearly yet.”

**Script outline:**
- “Autism parenting tip: there is a hidden sense called interoception.”
- “It helps us notice body signals like hunger, thirst, bathroom needs, pain, temperature, and overwhelm.”
- “Some autistic kids feel these signals late, too intensely, or not at all.”
- “That can look like sudden meltdowns, bathroom accidents, refusing food, or saying ‘I don’t know’ when asked what’s wrong.”
- “Try body check-ins with visuals instead of questions: hungry, thirsty, hot, cold, bathroom, tired, hurt.”
- “Behavior is information. The body may be speaking before the words can.”

**Suggested hashtags:**  
#Interoception #AutismTips #AutismParenting #BehaviorIsCommunication #Neurodiversity #AutisticKids #SensoryProcessing #SpectrumUnlocked

## 7. Trending/Timely: End-Of-School-Year Transition Survival Kit

**Post type:** Carousel  
**Hook/headline:** “May is transition season, and autistic kids often feel it before they can explain it.”

**Key points:**
1. End-of-year changes can disrupt predictability: field days, substitute teachers, parties, testing, schedule changes.
2. Watch for stress signs: sleep changes, more scripting, clinginess, meltdowns, refusal, stomachaches.
3. Ask school for a simple countdown calendar or weekly preview.
4. Make a “last week of school” visual schedule.
5. Preview summer routines before school ends.
6. Create one anchor that stays the same every day.
7. CTA: “Save this before the last week sneaks up.”
