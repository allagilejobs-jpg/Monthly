## 1. Educational - After-School Restraint Collapse Is Real

- **Post type:** Carousel
- **Hook/headline:** Your child is not "being difficult" after school. They may be finally safe enough to fall apart.
- **Key points:**
  1. Define after-school restraint collapse in plain language.
  2. Explain why school masking drains autistic kids.
  3. Signs parents may notice: tears, aggression, shutdown, snack refusal, sensory seeking.
  4. What helps: quiet transition, food and water, no questions for 20 minutes, dim lights, predictable decompression routine.
  5. What does not help: immediate homework, rapid questions, consequences before regulation.
  6. CTA: Comment "after school" if this sounds familiar.
- **Suggested hashtags:** #AutismParent #AfterSchoolMeltdown #AutismSupport #NeurodivergentKids #AutismMom #AutismDad #SensoryNeeds #AutismParenting

## 2. Educational - The 5-Minute Meltdown Debrief

- **Post type:** Reel
- **Hook/headline:** Do this after the meltdown, not during it.
- **Script outline:**
  - Opening: "During a meltdown, your job is safety and regulation. The teaching comes later."
  - Step 1: Wait until the child is fully calm, not just quiet.
  - Step 2: Write down the basics: time, place, trigger guess, body clues, what helped.
  - Step 3: Look for patterns over days, not blame in the moment.
  - Step 4: Use the pattern to change the environment next time.
  - Soft tool plug: "This is exactly why a meltdown tracker helps."
- **Suggested hashtags:** #MeltdownSupport #AutismParent #AutismTips #BehaviorIsCommunication #AutismFamily #Neurodiversity #ParentingTools #SpectrumUnlocked
