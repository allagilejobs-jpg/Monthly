# Content Research - April 15, 2026

## 🔥 Trending Topics This Week

### 1. Transition to Adulthood (HOT TOPIC)
Multiple news outlets covering the "cliff" that happens when autistic teens age out of school services. The limited availability of adult services is a major concern for parents.
- **Angle**: "What happens at 22?" - The transition cliff explained
- **Source**: Click on Detroit, Autism Spectrum News (Winter 2026 issue themed "Navigating Transitions Across the Lifespan")

### 2. Awareness vs Acceptance vs ACTION
The autism community is debating moving beyond both awareness AND acceptance to actual ACTION. "Autism Action Month" is gaining traction.
- **Angle**: "Stop talking, start doing" - What autism ACTION looks like
- **Source**: The Autism Dad blog, Autism Society

### 3. Late Diagnosis in Women/Girls
Growing coverage of how diagnostic criteria historically missed girls/women. 300% increase in diagnoses over past 20 years attributed to better awareness.
- **Angle**: "The missed generation" - Why many women are discovering autism in adulthood
- **Source**: J Weekly opinion piece, USC Annenberg

### 4. Belonging Over Fixing
Shift from "fixing" autism to creating environments where autistic people belong. Dr. Kirk Adams (ISDI) framing autism as "a complex, fully human way of experiencing the world."
- **Angle**: Your child doesn't need fixing - they need belonging
- **Source**: Institute for Sustainable Diversity & Inclusion

## 💡 Fresh Content Angles

### 1. "The IEP Translator" Series
Parents consistently struggling with IEP jargon. Create visual explainers for common IEP terms and what to actually ask for.
- Trending on r/Autism_Parenting: "Nervous about upcoming IEP meeting"
- Key asks: sensory room access, nonverbal communication supports

### 2. Employment Readiness (Age 14+)
Content gap: Most autism parenting content focuses on young children. Transition-age content (14-22) is underserved.
- Vocational skills, job coaching, self-advocacy training
- "What my teen needs to know before 22"

### 3. Sensory-First Approach
Refresh on sensory content - frame as PREVENTATIVE not reactive
- "Sensory diet" daily routines
- Recognizing sensory overload BEFORE meltdown
- Calming strategies with visuals

### 4. Black Autism Content
Underrepresented niche within autism parenting
- Hashtag: #BlackAndAutistic is growing
- Cultural considerations in diagnosis and therapy
- Finding culturally competent providers

### 5. "Actually Autistic" Parent Perspective
Growing audience of parents who are themselves autistic
- "Autistic parent raising autistic child"
- Unique challenges and strengths
