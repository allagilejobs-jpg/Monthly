# Content Research - May 6, 2026

## 🔥 Trending Topics This Week

### 1. Caregiver Well-Being After Autism Acceptance Month (TOP PICK)
WHO used its 2026 World Autism Awareness Day page to point people toward a May 5 launch/webinar for a new caregiver well-being training for children with developmental delays and neurodevelopmental conditions. This lands perfectly with May Mental Health Awareness Month and the post-April emotional crash many autism parents feel.

**Why it matters for SU:** Competitors often talk about parent burnout as generic self-care. The stronger angle is: caregiver well-being is not a luxury, it is part of the child’s support system.

**Content opportunities:**
- "Caregiver burnout is not a personal failure, it is a support gap"
- "After Autism Acceptance Month: why parents feel drained in May"
- "Your child's plan needs a parent support plan too"
- "5 tiny recovery supports for autism parents who cannot take a day off"

### 2. PDA, School Refusal, and Demand Avoidance Are Heating Up Again
Autism Parenting Magazine's May 2026 summit is promoting sessions around PDA vs. defiance and why children refuse. Search results around PDA school struggles, refusal, anxiety, sensory overload, and executive functioning are prominent.

**Why it matters for SU:** We have covered IEPs, meltdowns, visual supports, and ABA, but not a dedicated parent-friendly PDA/school refusal explainer.

**Content opportunities:**
- "Refusal is not always defiance: 5 hidden reasons your child says no"
- "PDA vs. power struggle: what parents should notice before escalating"
- "School refusal scripts that lower threat instead of raising demands"

### 3. Communication Access: Apraxia, AAC, and Speech-Language Month
May is a strong communication-access window: Apraxia Awareness Month/Day on May 14, plus National Speech-Language-Hearing Month. This bridges well with autism parenting because many families are navigating speech delays, AAC, motor planning, gestalt language, and school communication goals.

**Content opportunities:**
- "My child understands more than they can say"
- "AAC does not stop speech, it supports communication"
- "Apraxia is not stubbornness: what motor planning means"
- "IEP goal rewrite: from 'uses words' to 'uses reliable communication'"

### 4. Accessibility Beyond Ramps: GAAD on May 21
Global Accessibility Awareness Day is May 21, 2026. The official theme is digital access and inclusion, but SU can translate it into parent-relevant access: AAC apps, visual supports, school portals, sensory-friendly digital learning, and accessible content.

**Content opportunities:**
- "Accessibility is not just ramps: 6 access needs autistic kids may have"
- "If the school app is inaccessible, parents lose access too"
- "Digital accessibility for AAC users: what families should ask"

## 💡 Fresh Content Angles

### 1. "The Parent Support Plan"
Instead of another self-care post, frame caregiver well-being as infrastructure.

**Carousel structure:**
1. Your child's support plan is incomplete if it ignores the caregiver
2. Burnout changes how much regulation you can lend
3. Support can be tiny: meals, respite, scripts, one predictable break
4. Add it to the family plan, not the guilt list
5. CTA: "What support would actually help this week?"

### 2. "Refusal Is Communication"
Use PDA/school refusal interest without overdiagnosing. Keep it parent-safe: refusal may reflect anxiety, uncertainty, sensory overload, executive functioning, communication limits, or past demand trauma.

**Post ideas:**
- "Before you call it defiance, check these 6 things"
- "How to lower the threat level of a demand"
- "When 'no' means 'I can't access this yet'"

### 3. "Communication Is Bigger Than Speech"
Tie Apraxia Awareness Day and Speech-Language-Hearing Month to autism families.

**Post ideas:**
- "Speech is one way to communicate, not the only valid way"
- "AAC myths that delay support"
- "What to ask the SLP when your child has lots to say but cannot get words out"

### 4. "Access Audit for Home and School"
For GAAD, make accessibility tangible.

**Post ideas:**
- "Do a 10-minute accessibility audit of your child's school day"
- "Can your child ask for help, a break, water, pain relief, or the bathroom?"
- "Access means the child can participate without pretending not to need support"
