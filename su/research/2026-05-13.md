# Content Research - May 13, 2026

## 🔥 Trending Topics This Week

### 1. Communication Access Is Bigger Than Speech (TOP PICK)
May has a strong communication window: National Speech-Language-Hearing Month is active all month, Apraxia Awareness Day is tomorrow, and ASHA's May 2026 updates include caregiver concerns around communication breakdowns, summer support, and feeding challenges. TikTok discovery snippets around autism continue to surface `#autism`, `#neurodivergent`, autism acceptance activities, stimming, and daily neurodivergent experiences.

**Why it matters for SU:** Autism parenting content often treats speech as the finish line. A stronger, affirming angle is that communication access includes AAC, gestures, behavior, scripts, visual supports, pain/body signals, feeding needs, and break requests.

**Content opportunities:**
- "My child understands more than they can say"
- "AAC is not giving up on speech, it is giving your child access"
- "5 messages every autistic child needs a reliable way to communicate"
- "When behavior is the only available language"

### 2. PDA, Refusal, and School Struggles Continue to Heat Up
Autism Parenting Magazine's May 2026 summit is promoting parent pain points like PDA vs. defiance, refusal, behavior, speech, sensory issues, education, parent self-care, and mental health. Search results continue to show demand avoidance and school struggles as a recurring competitor topic.

**Why it matters for SU:** SU has covered IEPs, meltdowns, ABA, masking, sensory supports, and visual schedules, but still needs a dedicated parent-safe demand avoidance/school refusal lane.

**Content opportunities:**
- "Before you call it defiance, check these 6 access barriers"
- "School refusal is not always refusal: anxiety, sensory overload, demand threat, communication, or burnout"
- "How to lower the threat level of a demand without dropping the boundary"
- "What to ask before consequences: can they do this today, in this body, in this environment?"

### 3. Feeding and Picky Eating Are Freshly Relevant
ASHA posted a May 5 update about a texting program offering expert advice for picky eating and feeding challenges. This is a useful fresh hook because feeding often gets flattened into "picky" behavior, while autism families may be dealing with sensory aversion, oral-motor issues, interoception, rigidity, anxiety, GI discomfort, or unsafe mealtime pressure.

**Why it matters for SU:** Competitors cover feeding, but SU can connect it to sensory safety and communication, not just meal tips.

**Content opportunities:**
- "Picky eating or protected eating? 6 reasons autistic kids avoid foods"
- "What not to say at the table when food already feels unsafe"
- "Feeding support should reduce fear, not increase compliance"

### 4. Accessibility Beyond April
Global Accessibility Awareness Day is May 21, 2026. GAAD focuses on digital access and inclusion for more than one billion people with disabilities. SU can translate digital accessibility into parent-relevant access: AAC apps, captioned videos, school portals, visual supports, plain-language instructions, and low-clutter learning tools.

**Why it matters for SU:** This keeps acceptance content alive after April and moves from awareness language into practical access.

**Content opportunities:**
- "Accessibility is not just ramps: what access looks like for autistic kids"
- "Can your child ask for help, pain relief, the bathroom, water, or a break?"
- "A 10-minute access audit for home and school"

## 💡 Fresh Content Angles

### 1. "Communication Access Checklist"
Make this a highly saveable carousel or short reel.

**Hook:** "If your child cannot say it, they still need a way to communicate it."

**Checklist items:**
- I need a break
- I am in pain
- I need the bathroom
- This is too loud/bright/scratchy
- I do not understand the instruction
- I am hungry/thirsty
- I feel unsafe
- I need help, but I cannot explain why

**CTA:** "Which one does your child need more support communicating?"

### 2. "Protected Eating, Not Picky Eating"
Use feeding as a sensory and safety topic rather than a compliance topic.

**Hook:** "Some autistic kids are not being difficult at meals. They are protecting their body."

**Structure:** sensory texture, smell, predictability, pain/GI discomfort, oral-motor fatigue, demand pressure, then a gentle support step.

### 3. "The Demand Ladder"
Fresh PDA/school-refusal angle without overdiagnosing.

**Hook:** "A demand can feel tiny to you and huge to your child."

**Structure:** show how demands stack: transition + noise + uncertainty + language processing + fear of failure + fatigue. End with scripts that lower threat: "First we make it smaller," "Show me where it gets hard," "You can choose how we start."

### 4. "Access Audit for GAAD"
Practical and timely for May 21.

**Hook:** "Accessibility means your child can participate without pretending not to need support."

**Audit prompts:** school app, homework platform, classroom noise, captions, visual instructions, AAC availability, break card, substitute teacher plan, emergency communication.
