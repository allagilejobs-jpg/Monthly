## Email 2: Day 2
**Subject:** The one call you should make this week

Hi {first_name},

Quick check-in: how are you doing?

If you've had a chance to look at the guide, you might be feeling overwhelmed by all the "next steps." 

Let me simplify it:

**This week, make ONE call: your pediatrician.**

Ask them for referrals to:
- A developmental pediatrician (if you haven't seen one)
- Speech therapy
- Occupational therapy

Then get on those waitlists immediately. Even if you're not sure you need them. Waitlists can be 6-12 months long, and you can always decline later.

That's it. One call. One action. You've got this.

Tomorrow, I'll share the biggest mistake I made after our diagnosis (so you don't have to make it too).

💙 [Your name]

## Email 3: Day 4
**Subject:** The mistake I made (please don't repeat it)

Hi {first_name},

When my child was diagnosed, I made a mistake that cost us nearly a year of progress.

**I waited.**

I waited to see if things would "improve on their own."
I waited because the waitlists seemed too long.
I waited because I was still processing.

By the time we finally started therapy, we were almost a year behind where we could have been.

Early intervention matters. The research is clear: the earlier kids start getting support, the better their outcomes.

So here's my advice: don't wait.

- Get on every waitlist now (you can always say no later)
- Contact your school district about evaluations
- Start learning about IEPs and 504 plans

I've put together a list of the most important resources for each step. Reply "RESOURCES" and I'll send it over.

You're doing great. Keep going.

💙 [Your name]

## Email 4: Day 7
**Subject:** You need a village (here's how to find one)

Hi {first_name},

One week in. How are you holding up?

Here's something I've learned: **you can't do this alone.**

Not because you're not capable — you absolutely are. But because parenting a child with autism requires support. For your child AND for you.

Here's where to find your village:

**Online:**
- Facebook groups for autism parents (search your state + "autism parents")
- Reddit: r/autism and r/autismparents
- Instagram: follow #autismparent for community

**Local:**
- Ask your pediatrician about local support groups
- Check if your therapy providers offer parent groups
- Look for Autism Society chapters in your area

**At Spectrum Unlocked:**
- Our resource hub (always free)
- Coming soon: parent community & workshops

The parents who've walked this path before you? They're your greatest resource. They know the good therapists, the IEP tricks, the sensory hacks that actually work.

Find them. Learn from them. Let them support you.

💙 [Your name]

## Email 5: Day 10
**Subject:** What I want you to remember

Hi {first_name},

We've been emailing for about a week now, and I want to leave you with something important.

**Your child is the same amazing kid they were before the diagnosis.**

The diagnosis didn't change them. It gave you information. A lens. A way to understand how they experience the world.

And with that understanding comes power:
- The power to advocate for what they need
- The power to find therapies that actually help
- The power to build a life where they can thrive

There will be hard days. Days when the system fails you. Days when you're exhausted. Days when you wonder if you're doing enough.

On those days, remember:
- Progress isn't always linear
- You're learning a whole new world — be patient with yourself
- Your child doesn't need a perfect parent; they need a present one

I'm here whenever you need resources, support, or just someone who gets it.
