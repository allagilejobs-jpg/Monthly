🧠 Sensory Hacks

10 "Tuesday Tips" posts about sensory strategies for autism parents — ready to post!

📋 How to Use This Series

These 10 sensory hacks are designed for weekly "Tuesday Tips" posts. Each one includes:

  • ✅ Ready-to-post PNG image (1080×1920 story format)
  • ✅ Editable HTML template

Posting schedule: One hack per Tuesday = 10 weeks of content!

HACK #1

🎧 The "Noise Buffer" Strategy

Keep noise-canceling headphones in your bag at ALL times. Even without music, they cut sound by 20-30dB.

HACK #2

🛏️ Weighted Blanket Magic

Deep pressure input that calms. Use 10% of body weight. Even 10 minutes helps after a tough day.

HACK #3

🕶️ Sunglasses Indoors

Fluorescent lights can be overwhelming. Let your child wear sunglasses indoors — who cares what people think?

HACK #4

🌀 Fidget Rotation

Keep 3-4 different fidgets and rotate them weekly. The novelty keeps them effective longer.

HACK #5

⏱️ Transition Countdown

Give warnings at 5 minutes, 3 minutes, and 1 minute. Visual timers work even better than verbal.

HACK #6

🏕️ Create a Calm Corner

Dedicated calm-down spot at home. Small tent, bean bag, soft lighting. Not punishment — it's regulation.

HACK #7

🎒 Sensory Go-Bag

Pack headphones, fidgets, snacks, sunglasses, comfort item. Being prepared prevents meltdowns.

HACK #8

🤗 Compression Clothing

Tight undershirts, compression socks provide constant calming input. It's like a hug they can wear all day.

🌊 White Noise

White noise or nature sounds mask unpredictable noises that cause anxiety. Use at bedtime or during homework.

🍬 Oral Sensory Input
